# DGRV Digital Gap Assessment Tool - User Manual

## 1. Introduction
Welcome to the **DGRV Digital Gap Assessment Tool**. This platform is designed to help organizations and their member cooperatives identify "digital gaps"—areas where current digital capabilities do not yet meet desired targets—and provide actionable recommendations to bridge those gaps.

The application follows a structured hierarchy to ensure that assessments are consistent, professional, and strategically aligned with organization goals.

---

## 2. User Roles & Responsibilities

The application uses four distinct levels of access. Each level has specific tools tailored to their responsibility within the assessment ecosystem.

### 2.1 DGRV Admin (Global Level)
The DGRV Admin is the highest authority in the system. Their primary role is to set up the environment and define the "Rules of Engagement" for all participating organizations.

**Key Responsibilities:**
*   **Organization Management**: Registering new organizations on the platform and managing their details.
*   **Framework Definition (Dimensions)**: Creating the core categories of assessment (e.g., Digital Infrastructure, Employee Skills, Customer Experience).
*   **Organizational Setup**: Assigning specific assessment categories (Dimensions) to organizations based on their needs.
*   **Invitation Management**: Inviting the initial "Organization Admin" to join the platform for each registered organization.
*   **Gap Customization**: Defining global digital gaps and their severity levels to ensure a uniform standard across the platform.
*   **Consolidated Reporting**: Accessing high-level, aggregate reports across all organizations to identify cross-industry digital trends.

### 2.2 Organization Admin (Strategic Level)
The Organization Admin represents the organization's leadership. They are responsible for managing the assessment process specifically for their organization and its member cooperatives.

**Key Responsibilities:**
*   **Cooperative Management**: Creating internal groups or "Cooperatives" within the organization profile.
*   **User Management**: Inviting users to the organization and assigning them to specific cooperatives or administrative roles.
*   **Assessment Initiation**: Creating and naming a new "Assessment Session" (e.g., "Annual Digital Maturity Check 2024").
*   **Strategic Allocation**: Assigning specific assessment dimensions to the appropriate cooperatives or teams.
*   **Organization Reporting**: Generating and viewing consolidated reports that show the digital health of the entire organization and its member cooperatives.

### 2.3 Cooperative Admin (Operational Level)
The Cooperative Admin manages the assessment at the local level. They ensure that their cooperative’s team members are engaged and their assigned tasks are completed.

**Key Responsibilities:**
*   **Team Inclusion**: Identifying and adding members to their specific cooperative group within the system.
*   **Progress Oversight**: Monitoring which assigned assessment sections have been started, which are in progress, and which are completed.
*   **Engagement**: Coordinating with team members to ensure technical inputs are provided on time.

### 2.4 Cooperative User (Practitioner Level)
The Cooperative User is the "boots on the ground." They provide the actual data and insights required to measure the organization’s digital performance.

**Key Responsibilities:**
*   **Answering Assessments**: Responding to questions within the assigned categories.
*   **Defining States**: Describing the "Current State" (where we are now) and the "Desired State" (where we want to be) for specific digital capabilities.
*   **Providing Evidence**: Ensuring the descriptions are clear and accurate to help the system calculate the "Gap."
*   **Final Submission**: Submitting their dimension assessments once completed for final consolidation into the organization's report.

---

## 3. The Assessment Flow: Step-by-Step

Following this professional flow ensures that the final reports are accurate and useful.

### Step 1: Global Setup (DGRV Admin)
The journey begins with the DGRV Admin creating an **Organization** profile and defining the **Dimensions** (categories) that will be measured. They then assign these Dimensions to the Organization and invite the **Organization Admin**.

### Step 2: Local Structuring (Organization Admin)
The Organization Admin creates **Cooperatives** (groups) and invites members. They then set up an **Assessment Session** and decide which Cooperative is responsible for which Dimensions.

### Step 3: Data Entry (Cooperative User)
Assigned members (Cooperative Users) access their dashboard to see the categories assigned to them. They fill out descriptions for their current and desired digital maturity levels. The system will automatically calculate the "Gap" and suggest severity levels based on these inputs.

### Step 4: Consolidation (Automatic)
As members submit their individual sections, the system consolidates this data. It aggregates scores, calculates average risks, and identifies top recommendations.

### Step 5: Reporting & Insights (Admins)
*   **Cooperative Level**: View local summaries and action plans.
*   **Organization Level**: View the "Consolidated Org Report" to see performance across all cooperatives.
*   **DGRV Level**: Access the "Global Consolidated Report" for bird's-eye views and cross-org comparisons.

---

## 4. Professional Best Practices
*   **Be Explicit in Descriptions**: When defining your current state, provide concrete examples. This makes the generated recommendations much more effective.
*   **Review Roles Regularly**: Ensure that the right people have "Admin" access. Only those who need to generate reports or invite others should have administrative permissions.
*   **Timely Submission**: Try to complete assessments within the timeframe set by your Organization Admin to keep reporting cycles consistent.
